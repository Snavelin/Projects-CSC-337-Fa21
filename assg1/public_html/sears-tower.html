THE SEARS TOWER

Beginning construction in 1970 and completing four years later, the Sears Tower (now Willis Tower) is a 108-floor skyscraper that dominates the Chicago skyline from the southern edge of downtown. It surpassed the World Trade Center as the tallest building in the world and kept the title until 1998, when it was surpassed by the Petronas Towers.

It served as the headquarters of the retail company Sears, Roebuck and Co., the tower's namesake, from its opening to 1994. As a term of their lease of the building, the Willis Group had the tower's name changed to the Willis Tower.
