THE WILLIS TOWER

The Willis Group Holdings is a global insurance brokerage firm in the midwest. They originally leased space in the Sears Tower located on the southern edge of the Finance District in downtown Chicago. In 2009, the Sears Tower was renamed the Willis Tower. In that year, a new exhibit experience was built on the upper floors: the Skydeck and the Ledge, which gave tourists a unique view of Chicago and the surrounding states. Although the building retained the name of Willis Tower, the holding group sold it to the Blackstone Group for $1.3 billion in 2017. A major redevelopment quickly began in 2018. Besides modernization, the goal of the project was to make the building an urban center in of itself, combining work and play.

Who would've thought that a mail order company, Sears, Roebuck and Co., whose only goal was to consolidate their corporate offices into one building, could have been the origin of such a forward-thinking achievement?

Owners of the tower at 233 S Wacker Dr, Chicago, IL
